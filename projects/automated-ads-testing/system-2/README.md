## Lesson 2

- Small marginal creative testing and highly variable creative testing (do we want to isolate variables or do we want to try something new, the other guy says 80/20 where 80 is small marginal testing and 20 is highly variable testing)

- Small changes create small variations in outcome. Make bigger changes if you want the outcome to differ greatly, don't take David Ogilvy's advice (this is opposite of what strategy 1 said, I think)

- Again broad targeting is the way, Target different audiences by running different creative for the same broad audiences (different **angles**)

- How to come up with variation of creative?
  - Video vs still
  - Format: https://commonthreadco.com/blogs/coachs-corner/creative-facebook-ads-types-examples
  - Placement: instagram vs facebook, reels vs feed vs stories
  - Offer: first place to look if ads don't perfom
  - Angle: different benefits to highlight

Again, make bigger changes...

## Lesson 3: Quality

Direct response advertising vs brand advertising

Product is the hero of the ad

Clarity > everything

"Buy the premise, buy the bit": Give all required context up front so that when the punchline comes the customer is ready to "laugh".

Clarity starts with the image: "It isn't the whiskey they choose, it's the image"

Stills still work, but the photography has to make the product look beautiful, desireable and be clear what it is.

## Lesson 4: Quality

Stages of the ad: AIDA

A: Capture attention with a hook
I: Interest them with a benefits
D: Create desire for the product
A: Call them to action

It starts with words, great advertising is conceptual

Introduce the angle in the headline, have it pique interest and make sure you have absolute mental clarity on what you're trying to communicate

## Lesson 5

Explanatory content uniquely drives desire (reason based sales). For SEO content, explaining your product may be good

Headline: Attention & Interest with angle
Body: explanatory content of the product to drive desire by explaining the value of the product

Long copy can work as long as it is high value density in terms of driving desire

Video can be good because
- Clear product imagery
- Clear product headline
- Detailed explanatory content that stays engaging

Different formats of landing page (landing pages can also be A/B tested and also generated with AI)

4 ways of communicating information content (Work through these in order)
- explanation
- illustration
- application
- restatement
